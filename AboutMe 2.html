About Me

From Sales Floor
to Data Stories

I currently work in sales, where performance, targets, customer behavior, and execution matter every day. Over time, I found myself becoming just as interested in the numbers behind the results as the results themselves. That curiosity pushed me into data analytics, where I now focus on turning raw data into clear stories, useful insights, and better business decisions.

Quick Snapshot

  • Currently working in sales and leadership in a fast-paced retail environment
  • Built hands-on experience around KPIs, coaching, performance tracking, and business execution
  • Passionate about data analytics, SQL, Power BI, and turning numbers into business stories
  • Building projects around retail intelligence, reporting, and decision-making

My Story

I started on the sales floor. Numbers there aren’t abstract — they’re attached to real targets, real customers, and real consequences. Tracking attach rates, watching team performance shift day to day, trying to figure out why results moved. That environment built something in me before I even realized it was happening.

At some point I stopped being satisfied with just hitting the number. I wanted to know why it moved, where the opportunity was being left on the table, and what the data was actually saying underneath the result. That question didn’t leave me alone — and that’s what pulled me toward analytics.

What I Do Today

What I do today is still rooted in that same environment. I work across sales performance, customer experience, KPIs, margin, and team execution. But the part that really grabbed me was never the selling itself — it was understanding the business behind the sale. Why results were up or down. What patterns kept showing up. Where execution was slipping and nobody had connected the dots yet.

Why Data Analytics

That’s why data analytics. Not because it’s a career pivot — because it’s the natural next step of a question I’ve been asking since day one on the floor. I want to build work that doesn’t just report what happened. It explains why, and points to what should happen next.

What I Bring

Sales Perspective

I understand business performance from the floor level, where targets, customer decisions, and execution happen in real time.

Leadership Mindset

I think in terms of accountability, coaching, improvement, and how actions connect to results.

Analytical Curiosity

I naturally ask why numbers moved, what caused the change, and what the data is really saying.

Business Storytelling

I want reporting to be useful, clear, and tied to decisions — not just charts that look nice.

Where I’m Going

My goal is to move deeper into data analytics and business intelligence by combining the business side I already know with stronger technical skills in SQL, Power BI, data modeling, and reporting. I want to build work that helps teams understand performance clearly and make smarter decisions faster.
